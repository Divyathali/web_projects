Twitter introduces Birdwatch, will now let users flag misleading tweets

Twitter has introduced Birdwatch, a model to combat misinformation on the platform, that will let users flag tweets they think are misleading and add notes to help people understand and evaluate a tweet by reading the context on notes. Twitter allows anyone to sign up and participate in this program, and encourages a diverse group, to effectively address misinformation.

For now, users who can contribute to Birdwatch must have a verified phone and email, a trusted US-based phone carrier, two-factor authentication enabled and no recent notice of Twitter Rules violations.Keith Coleman, Vice President of Twitter Product, in a blog post wrote, "We believe this approach has the potential to respond quickly when misleading information spreads, adding context that people trust and find valuable." The pilot participants can flag tweets and add notes to tweets they think are misleading.

Career in Risk Management: Degrees and certifications, skills needed, salary

Risk management is the process of proactively identifying, analysing, and mitigating risks to ensure that an organization is able to successfully navigate them and achieve its business objectives. The term VUCA Volatile, Uncertain, Complex and Ambiguous is often used to describe the world we live in.

What this means is that we exist in a world that is not static it keeps evolving and, because economies across the globe are interconnected, events can occur that may have an impact beyond the immediate and foreseeable. Risk management processes allow organisations to predict emerging threats, understand the implications, and devise a strategy to avoid or minimize their repercussions.

Traditionally, risk management was limited in scope to finance and insurance. However, events like the financial crisis, the Covid-19 pandemic and multiple corporate failures, have brought the need and importance of enterprise risk management to the forefront.
